GEO questions answered plainly.

GEO is the practice of building the signals and sources that cause AI tools — ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI Overviews — to cite you as a credible, authoritative source in their generated responses. Unlike SEO, which optimises for ranking in a list of links, GEO optimises for being named in a conversational recommendation. It's the difference between appearing in a search result and being personally endorsed by an AI assistant.
ChatGPT draws from its training data (pre-2025 web content) and, in its browsing-enabled mode, live web searches. It prioritises names that appear repeatedly across credible, independent sources — property blogs, news articles, forums like Reddit, review sites, and professional directories. The more your name is associated with specific expertise and location across these sources, the more likely ChatGPT will surface you when someone asks.
Yes — and it's already happening for some agents. When ChatGPT is asked "who are the best HDB agents in Tampines?" or "which property agent should I use in Singapore?", it returns names from agents who have sufficient authoritative mentions in its source material. Individual agents can appear — and in fact, deep local expertise often gives them an advantage over large agencies whose mentions are scattered across too many locations.
SEO gets you ranked in a list of links. GEO gets you cited in a conversational recommendation. SEO is about your website's structure and content. GEO is about your presence across the wider web — the sources that AI reads to form opinions. They're different systems requiring different work, though a strong SEO foundation supports GEO by making your own site a credible source.
