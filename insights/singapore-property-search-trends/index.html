Singapore Property Search Trends — How Buyers and Sellers Are Searching in 2025

This report covers how Singapore residents search for property online — across Google, ChatGPT, and emerging AI platforms. All volume figures are estimates based on keyword research tools and Search Console benchmarking. For verified transaction data, refer to data.gov.sg and the HDB Resale Price Index.

Overview

Key Findings at a Glance

HDB resale searches have grown year-on-year — queries like "HDB resale price", "sell HDB" and estate-specific resale terms show consistent upward trend based on keyword tool data.

Estate-specific searches convert at higher rates than broad terms — a buyer searching "property agent Tampines" is closer to making contact than one searching "property agent Singapore."

AI search adoption among Singapore property buyers is accelerating in 2024–2025 — early indicators from ChatGPT and Perplexity usage show growing property-related query volumes.

Mobile accounts for the majority of property searches in Singapore — consistent with global mobile-first browsing trends and Singapore's high smartphone penetration rate.

Most property agents have no meaningful organic search presence — the majority of Google traffic goes to portals (PropertyGuru, 99.co) and a small number of SEO-active agents.

Figures above are directional estimates based on keyword research tools. For official transaction statistics, see data.gov.sg and the HDB Resale Price Index.

Search Behaviour

How Singaporeans Search for Property Online

Mobile vs Desktop Split

Singapore's smartphone penetration is among the highest in Southeast Asia. Property searches follow this pattern — the majority of queries happen on mobile devices, particularly for estate-level and agent searches. Desktop usage tends to be higher for longer research sessions, such as comparing resale prices or reading lengthy property listings.

For property agents, this means your website and content must load fast and be fully legible on mobile. A page that renders poorly on a phone loses leads before they read a single word.

The Search Journey from Awareness to Agent Contact

Most Singapore property buyers and sellers follow a multi-stage search journey. Early in the process, they search broad terms: "sell HDB Singapore", "HDB resale process", "when can I sell my HDB". As they get closer to acting, searches become estate-specific and agent-specific: "property agent Tampines", "best agent to sell HDB Bedok", "who is the top agent in Jurong."

Agents who only appear for the latter (high-intent) queries miss the majority of the journey. Those who appear consistently across the full funnel — from informational to commercial queries — build familiarity before the buyer is even ready to call.

Time-of-Day Search Patterns

Property searches in Singapore tend to peak in the evenings (8–11pm) and on weekends, reflecting that most buyers and sellers are working adults researching outside office hours. This has implications for when content is published and when Google Business Profile updates are made — recency and freshness matter for local search signals.

Property Types

Most-Searched Property Types in Singapore

HDB Resale

HDB resale searches dominate property-related search volume in Singapore by a wide margin, reflecting the scale of the resale market. Top queries include "HDB resale price", "sell my HDB flat", "HDB resale eligibility", and estate-specific terms like "4-room HDB Tampines resale." Estimated monthly searches for core HDB resale terms range from the low thousands to tens of thousands nationally.

The search competition for these terms is high at the national level but drops significantly at the estate and street level — the most under-served opportunity for property agents.

Condo and EC

Condo and Executive Condominium (EC) searches are driven by two distinct audiences: HDB upgraders researching EC eligibility and pricing, and investment buyers looking at private condominium returns. Key queries include "EC Singapore", "EC eligibility income ceiling", and development-specific terms (e.g. "Tampines North EC price", "Jurong condo for sale").

EC searches have particularly high commercial intent — buyers at this stage are typically closer to making a decision and more likely to contact an agent.

Landed

Landed property searches are lower volume but high value. Common queries include "landed property Singapore", "semi-detached for sale Bukit Timah", and "freehold terrace Singapore." The audience is typically high-net-worth or asset-rich HDB sellers making an upgrade. Most estate-level landed search terms have relatively low competition, making them achievable for specialist agents.

New Launch

New launch property searches spike around major development launches and are strongly driven by developer marketing. Agents benefit less from organic SEO for new launch terms since developers outspend on paid advertising. However, informational content around "new launch vs resale pros and cons" and "new launch process Singapore" still attracts buyers early in their research.

Locations

Most-Searched Property Locations in Singapore

East — Tampines, Bedok, Pasir Ris

The eastern region generates some of the highest estate-level property search volumes in Singapore. Tampines property search data shows strong demand across HDB resale, EC, and agent-specific queries, driven by the estate's size and active resale market. Bedok and Pasir Ris follow closely, with buyers searching for both resale prices and agent recommendations. Very few agents in this region have meaningful SEO presence despite high search volume.

West — Jurong, Bukit Batok, Clementi

Western estates see strong HDB resale search activity, particularly around Jurong. Jurong property search data reflects a mature market with consistent buyer and seller demand. Bukit Batok and Clementi have active search demand from HDB upgraders and investors, with Clementi benefiting from proximity to educational institutions. Agent-specific searches in this region are under-served relative to query volume.

North — Woodlands, Sembawang, Yishun

Northern estates are a high-volume, low-competition opportunity for property agents. Woodlands sees significant HDB resale search demand, particularly for larger flat types. Sembawang and Yishun are driven by a mix of first-time buyers researching affordability and HDB sellers approaching MOP. Local SEO and estate-level content in these areas face less established competition than the eastern and central regions.

Central — Orchard, Bukit Timah, Bishan

Central Singapore has distinct search dynamics. Orchard and Bukit Timah searches are dominated by private property and landed queries, while Bishan is more HDB-driven. Central region searches have higher commercial value per query but are more competitive. Specialist agents in the central private market benefit disproportionately from SEO — the buyer pool is smaller but transaction values are significantly higher.

AI Search Trends

The Rise of AI Search in Singapore Property Queries

How Many Buyers Are Using ChatGPT for Property Research?

Reliable figures on AI search adoption for Singapore property queries are not yet publicly available from primary sources. However, early indicators — including referral traffic patterns and anecdotal reports from agents and buyers — suggest a growing share of property research begins in ChatGPT or Perplexity, particularly for procedural questions (MOP calculations, CPF usage, resale eligibility) rather than agent discovery. This is an emerging trend, not yet at the scale of Google, but its trajectory is upward. Learn more about AI search optimisation for property agents.

What Questions Buyers Ask AI About Singapore Property

Based on observed query patterns, Singapore property buyers tend to ask AI platforms:

  • "How does HDB MOP work and when can I sell?"
  • "What is the EC income ceiling in Singapore?"
  • "How much do I need to pay an agent when selling my HDB?"
  • "What are the steps to buy a resale HDB flat?"
  • "Who are the top property agents in [estate] Singapore?"

The last query type is particularly significant for agents — it represents direct agent discovery through AI, and the agents cited by ChatGPT or Perplexity tend to be those with the most structured online presence.

Which AI Platforms Are Gaining Ground in Singapore

ChatGPT (OpenAI) remains the dominant AI search platform for Singapore users as of 2025, followed by Perplexity AI and Google's AI Overviews integrated into standard search results. Perplexity's citation model makes it especially valuable for agents — it actively links to sources, meaning well-positioned agents and content can be directly surfaced. Google AI Overviews affect how traditional SEO traffic converts by answering queries directly on the results page, reducing click-through rates for informational content.
