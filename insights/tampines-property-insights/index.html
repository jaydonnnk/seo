Tampines Property Insights — Market Data, Search Trends, and Agent Landscape 2026

A research overview of the Tampines property market — covering HDB resale activity, EC demand, search behaviour, and the agent landscape. All price and volume figures are estimates clearly marked as such. For verified transaction data, refer to the HDB Resale Price Index and data.gov.sg.

Overview

Tampines at a Glance — Key Market Facts

One of Singapore's largest HDB estates — estimated 60,000+ HDB dwelling units across Tampines, making it one of the most active resale markets in the country.

Predominantly HDB with EC presence — property types include HDB flats (majority), EC developments such as Tampines Trilliant and Tampines North EC, and limited private condominium stock.

Large, stable household base — Tampines GRC and SMC cover a dense residential population with strong community infrastructure, Tampines Regional Centre employment, and major retail anchors (Tampines Mall, Tampines 1, Century Square).

Active buyer and seller base year-round — combination of MOP units entering the market, EC upgraders, and cross-estate buyers from Bedok and Pasir Ris creates consistent transaction volume.

Unit counts and population figures are estimates. Verify current data via HDB's InfoWEB, data.gov.sg, or the Singapore Department of Statistics.

Fastest-Transacting Streets and Blocks

Areas around Tampines MRT (Tampines Ave 1, Ave 3, Ave 5) and Tampines West MRT (Tampines St 81, St 84) tend to see higher transaction velocity due to MRT proximity. Blocks near Tampines Regional Centre and major bus interchanges also attract consistent buyer interest. For block-level transaction data, the HDB Resale Flat Prices dataset on data.gov.sg provides the most granular view.

HDB MOP Units and Pipeline Supply Context

Tampines has been an active BTO launching area, which means a steady pipeline of HDB flats reaching Minimum Occupation Period (MOP) and entering the resale market each year. Agents tracking which BTO projects are approaching their MOP date have an advantage in proactively reaching sellers before they list with a competitor. MOP dates are available from the original BTO project records — typically 5 years from key collection date.

Condo and EC

Tampines Condo and EC Market 2026

Key EC Developments and Recent Transaction Context

Tampines Trilliant (launched 2011, privatised) and Tampines North EC (more recent launch) are the most significant EC developments in the area. Both have attracted buyers from across the eastern corridor. EC transactions in Tampines are closely watched by agents because they often signal the next wave of HDB upgraders — EC buyers frequently sell their HDB flat as part of the upgrade, creating a two-sided transaction opportunity for a single agent relationship.

For verified EC transaction prices and volumes, refer to URA REALIS or the SRX price index.

EC Eligibility and Demand Profile

EC buyers in Singapore must meet HDB eligibility criteria including citizenship, income ceiling (currently $16,000/month for the household), and first-timer status (or applicable second-timer conditions). The Tampines EC buyer profile tends to be dual-income households aged 28–40, often with one or two children, looking to upgrade from a resale HDB or a BTO flat reaching MOP.

Agents serving this segment need content that addresses the full EC journey — from "EC eligibility check" (a frequently searched query) through to the financial comparison against resale condos.

Price Comparison vs Nearby Districts

Tampines EC and private condo prices are generally benchmarked against the broader eastern corridor. Nearby districts (Bedok, Pasir Ris, Sengkang) offer comparable or slightly lower private property prices, while Paya Lebar and Geylang command premiums for MRT accessibility. Agents positioned as Tampines specialists can leverage this regional price context to educate buyers on value positioning.

What Buyers Are Asking ChatGPT About Tampines

  • "Is Tampines a good estate to buy HDB resale in 2026?"
  • "What is the average price of a 4-room HDB in Tampines?"

Mobile Search Patterns

Like most Singapore property searches, Tampines queries are predominantly mobile. Evening peak (8–11pm) and weekend activity is higher than working hours, consistent with buyers and sellers researching after work. Pages that load slowly or are not mobile-optimised lose these visitors before they read past the headline.

Competition

The Tampines Property Agent Landscape

Estimated Number of Active Agents in Tampines

Singapore has over 30,000 licensed property agents registered with the CEA (Council for Estate Agencies). A meaningful proportion transact in Tampines, given its scale, but the number of agents who specialise and actively market themselves as Tampines specialists is far smaller. For an exact count of CEA-registered agents active in Tampines, the CEA public register is the authoritative source.

What Types of Agents Serve the Tampines Market

Tampines agents generally fall into three categories:

HDB Resale Specialists

Agents who focus primarily on 4-room and 5-room HDB resale transactions in Tampines and adjacent estates. The largest segment by volume.

HDB-to-EC or HDB-to-Condo Upgrader Agents

Agents skilled at managing the simultaneous sale of an existing HDB flat and purchase of an EC or private condo — a complex transaction requiring coordination across two timelines.

General-Market Agents Active in Tampines

Agents based across Singapore who transact occasionally in Tampines as part of a broad geographic coverage. Less likely to have deep estate knowledge or a referral network in the area.

Which Agents Are Visible Online vs Invisible

The majority of Tampines agents are invisible online — no dedicated website, no Google Business Profile optimised for Tampines, no estate-specific content. Their digital footprint consists of portal listings (PropertyGuru, 99.co) which they do not control, and social media posts that disappear from feeds. A small number of agents have built actual online presence — and those agents capture a disproportionate share of direct inbound leads.
