01 — Understanding Valuation

How Property Valuation Works in Singapore

HDB Valuation vs Market Value

HDB's official valuation is the figure a bank uses to determine your loan quantum and the CPF amount you can use. The market value is what a willing buyer will actually pay — which can be higher (cash-over-valuation, or COV) or, in a slower market, equal to valuation. In Jurong West and Jurong East, well-located 4-room and 5-room flats have commanded COV of $15,000–$60,000 in active market conditions. Understanding the gap is essential before you price your listing.

Who Determines Your Property's Valuation

For HDB resale transactions, the official valuation is issued by HDB through a panel of licensed valuers. The buyer's bank commissions this valuation after an OTP is exercised. For private property and EC resale, the bank appoints an independent valuer from its panel. In all cases, the valuation is based on recent comparable transactions (comps) — properties of similar type, size, and location that have sold within the recent period. This is why local comparable data from a Jurong agent is more accurate than any algorithm.

Online Estimates vs Official HDB Valuations

Property portals and online estimate tools use HDB's public transaction data to generate price estimates. These are useful for ballpark figures but lag the market by weeks or months, and don't account for your flat's specific condition, renovation premium, or block-specific desirability. HDB's own e-Valuation service provides an indicative valuation based on recent transactions in your precinct — this is the most accurate freely available estimate. A Jurong agent with live transaction data will be more precise still, especially given how significantly JLD proximity affects values across different Jurong streets.

Price Trend

Market direction in Jurong

HDB resale prices across Jurong West and Jurong East have held up well in 2024–2025 following cooling measures, with upgrader demand from MOP sellers sustaining transaction volumes. Blocks near Jurong East MRT and the Westgate/JEM retail belt continue to attract stronger buyer interest than the outer Jurong West precincts.

Private condo and new launch values near Jurong Lake District are trending upward as JLD infrastructure matures. The Jurong Region Line (JRL) adds medium-term upside to values along its corridor, particularly for Boon Lay, Jurong West, and Tengah-adjacent developments.

03 — What Drives Value

Factors That Affect Your Jurong Property's Value

🏢

Floor Level and Block Orientation

Higher floors command a premium in Jurong — typically $8,000–$25,000 per floor band depending on view and sunlight direction. Units with Jurong Lake or reservoir views attract a meaningful premium over equivalent floors facing interior courtyards. West-facing units in Jurong can suffer significant afternoon heat due to the low western sun — north-south orientation is most desirable. Corner units often trade at a premium. These micro-factors are routinely ignored by online estimates but priced in immediately by active buyers.

📅

Remaining Lease and Age of Flat

HDB flats with fewer than 60 years of remaining lease face CPF usage restrictions that significantly reduce the buyer pool and achievable price. Jurong has a mix of mature-estate blocks (Jurong East, Boon Lay) that are older and require lease verification. Most Jurong West blocks from the 1990s onwards have comfortable remaining lease of 65–80+ years, but first-generation Jurong East blocks from the 1970s–80s may be approaching the threshold where bank financing terms tighten.

🚇

Proximity to MRT, Schools, and Amenities

The premium for proximity to Jurong East MRT (EWL/NSL interchange) and Pioneer MRT is consistently reflected in transaction data — units within 400m typically transact 5–10% above comparable units further away. The Jurong Region Line (JRL) adds new value corridors along Jurong West. School proximity (Rulang Primary, Jurong Primary, Pioneer Primary) drives a buyer premium. Being near Jurong Point, IMM, JEM, or Westgate adds convenience appeal that directly shortens sale timelines.

📊

Recent Transactions in Your Street or Block

The most reliable predictor of your flat's value is the most recent comparable sale in your block or street — same flat type, similar floor range, recent date. This data is publicly available on HDB's resale transaction portal and gives you a defensible anchor for both listing price and COV expectations. A Jurong agent with active listings will have this data current to within days, not months — and will know which Jurong West streets are currently outperforming and which are moving slowly.

04 — Getting Your Valuation

How to Get an Accurate Valuation for Your Jurong Property

STEP 1

Use HDB's e-Valuation Service

HDB's e-Services portal provides an indicative valuation for your specific flat — block, unit, and flat type — based on recent resale transactions in your precinct. This is the closest freely available proxy for the official bank valuation figure. It's a useful starting point and gives you a sense of where your flat sits relative to recent comparable sales, but it won't account for your flat's specific condition, renovation, or the current buyer demand environment in Jurong's different sub-precincts.

STEP 2

Get a Comparative Market Analysis from an Agent

A CMA from a Jurong specialist agent gives you the most actionable picture: your flat's expected listing price, the COV you can realistically target in current market conditions, and a comparison against the most recent sales in your block. A good agent will also advise on whether JLD proximity meaningfully affects your unit's positioning in buyer searches and whether any renovation would yield a return. Connect with a Jurong property specialist for a no-obligation CMA.

STEP 3

Commission a Formal Bank Valuation When Needed

A formal bank valuation (typically $250–$500) is required when you're refinancing, when a buyer's bank finances the purchase, or when you need a legally defensible figure for estate or legal purposes. This is conducted by a panel valuer appointed by the financial institution. You don't typically need to commission this independently — it's triggered as part of a loan application or resale transaction. If you're refinancing using home equity, ask your bank to initiate the process.
