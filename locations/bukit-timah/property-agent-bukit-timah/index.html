02 — Agent Selection

What to Look for in a Bukit Timah Property Agent

Landed and GCB Transaction History

Ask specifically for landed closings in Bukit Timah in the past 24 months — ideally GCBs, semi-Ds, or high-value terraces. An agent who has completed prestige landed transactions knows the GCB eligibility check, the title search nuances, the valuation methodology, and the buyer vetting process that prevents wasted time on unqualified enquiries. Track record here is not negotiable.

School Catchment and Timing Expertise

A significant portion of Bukit Timah buyers are purchasing to secure catchment for Nanyang Primary, Methodist Girls' School, or Raffles Girls' Primary. An agent who understands the registration timeline — MOE Phase 2A and 2B, the 1km radius boundary, and the minimum occupancy period before registration — can prevent a buyer from making a $4M purchase that doesn't achieve its primary purpose.

HNW Buyer Network and Discretion

GCB and high-value landed transactions are rarely public-facing. Sellers at this tier often require discretion — off-market listings, private viewing arrangements, and buyer vetting before any details are shared. An agent with an active high-net-worth network can match a GCB to a qualified Singapore Citizen buyer without a portal listing and without compromising the seller's privacy or price expectations.

The landed-vs-condo decision in Bukit Timah turns on several factors. Landed property offers land ownership, no MCST fees, and the ability to rebuild or add extensions — but requires more active maintenance, involves higher stamp duty at the top end, and for GCBs, is restricted to Singapore Citizens. Condos in Bukit Timah — particularly along Sixth Avenue and in the Clementi Park area — offer maintenance-free living, stronger rental yield profiles, and are accessible to PRs and foreigners (with ABSD).
