Why Orchard Demands a Specialist, Not a Generalist

Orchard at a glance — low volume, maximum complexity

Ardmore and Claymore are ultra-luxury freehold, where prices reach $4,500–$6,000 psf and buyers include family offices, ultra-high-net-worth individuals, and foreign nationals willing to absorb 60% ABSD. River Valley and Leonie Hill skew toward expat rental demand, with sub-3,000 sqft units and yields that attract institutional landlords. Tanglin and Grange target the HNW upgrader transitioning from landed.

A generalist agent treats all of these as "Orchard property". 02 — Agent Selection

What to Look for in an Orchard Property Agent

Luxury Condo Transaction History

Ask specifically for Orchard condo closings above $3M in the past 24 months. An agent with five completed luxury deals knows this buyer profile from experience — not theory. They've handled the due diligence, the legal timeline, the cash-versus-CPF split, and the negotiation dynamics unique to high-value transactions in Districts 9 and 10.

ABSD and Foreign Buyer Expertise

Orchard's buyer pool includes a significant share of foreign nationals and permanent residents, each with different ABSD exposure. An agent who understands decoupling strategies, trust structures, and the true all-in cost for a foreign buyer can position your listing appropriately — and avoid the dead-end of marketing a $8M unit to buyers who can't close because of stamp duty miscalculation.

Freehold and En-Bloc Awareness

Many Orchard condos carry en-bloc potential — a factor that materially affects price strategy for sellers. An agent with freehold and en-bloc experience knows which developments are at the threshold for collective sale consent, how to factor en-bloc optionality into asking price, and how to communicate that potential to investor buyers who value the exit pathway.
