01 — Understanding Valuation

How Property Valuation Works in Singapore

HDB Valuation vs Market Value

HDB's official valuation is the figure a bank uses to determine your loan quantum and the CPF amount you can use. The market value is what a willing buyer will actually pay — which can be higher (cash-over-valuation, or COV) or, in a slower market, equal to valuation. In Tampines, well-located 4-room and 5-room flats have commanded COV of $20,000–$80,000 in active market conditions. Understanding the gap is essential before you price your listing.

Who Determines Your Property's Valuation

For HDB resale transactions, the official valuation is issued by HDB through a panel of licensed valuers. The buyer's bank commissions this valuation after an OTP is exercised. For private property and EC resale, the bank appoints an independent valuer from its panel. In all cases, the valuation is based on recent comparable transactions (comps) — properties of similar type, size, and location that have sold within the recent period. This is why local comparable data from an agent is more accurate than any algorithm.

Online Estimates vs Official HDB Valuations

Property portals and online estimate tools use HDB's public transaction data to generate price estimates. These are useful for ballpark figures but lag the market by weeks or months, and don't account for your flat's specific condition, renovation premium, or block-specific desirability. HDB's own e-Valuation service provides an indicative valuation based on recent transactions in your precinct — this is the most accurate freely available estimate. A Tampines agent with live transaction data will be more precise still.

Price Trend

Market direction in Tampines

HDB resale prices in Tampines have remained resilient in 2024–2025 following cooling measures, with well-located flats maintaining values close to 2023 peaks. The market has not softened significantly in Tampines due to persistent upgrader demand and limited new HDB supply in the estate.

Private condo prices are broadly stable with select developments — particularly those near MRT or in good school belts — continuing to see modest appreciation. The upcoming Cross Island Line connection adds long-term upside to Tampines property values.

03 — What Drives Value

Factors That Affect Your Tampines Property's Value

🏢

Floor Level and Block Orientation

Higher floors command a premium in Tampines — typically $10,000–$30,000 per floor band depending on view and sunlight direction. East-facing units get morning sun; west-facing units attract afternoon heat. North-south facing is generally most desirable for HDB buyers. Corner units often trade at a premium due to additional windows and ventilation. These are micro-factors that online estimates ignore but that local buyers price in immediately.

📅

Remaining Lease and Age of Flat

HDB flats with fewer than 60 years of remaining lease face CPF usage restrictions that significantly reduce the buyer pool and, therefore, achievable price. Most Tampines HDB flats have comfortable remaining lease (70–85+ years depending on TOP year), but this warrants verification. Older blocks in Tampines Central may be approaching the range where lease remaining begins to affect bank financing terms.

🚇

Proximity to MRT, Schools, and Amenities

The premium for proximity to Tampines MRT (EWL) and Tampines West MRT (DTL) is consistently reflected in transaction prices — units within 400m of an MRT station typically transact 5–10% above comparable units further away. Primary school proximity (St. Hilda's Primary, Poi Ching School) also drives a buyer premium. Proximity to Tampines Mall, Century Square, and Tampines Hub adds convenience appeal that translates to faster sale times.

📊

Recent Transactions in Your Street or Block

The most reliable predictor of your flat's value is the most recent comparable sale in your block or street — same flat type, similar floor range, recent date. This data is publicly available on HDB's resale transaction portal and gives you a defensible anchor for both listing price and COV expectations. An agent with active Tampines listings will have this data current to within days, not months.

04 — Getting Your Valuation

How to Get an Accurate Valuation for Your Tampines Property

STEP 1

Use HDB's e-Valuation Service

HDB's e-Services portal provides an indicative valuation for your specific flat — block, unit, and flat type — based on recent resale transactions in your precinct. This is the closest freely available proxy for the official bank valuation figure. It's a useful starting point and gives you a sense of where your flat sits relative to recent comparable sales, but it won't account for your flat's specific condition, renovation, or the current buyer demand environment.

STEP 2

Get a Comparative Market Analysis from an Agent

A CMA from a Tampines specialist agent gives you the most actionable picture: your flat's expected listing price, the COV you can realistically target in current market conditions, and a comparison against the most recent sales in your block. A good agent will also advise on whether any renovation improvements would yield a meaningful return versus market expectation. Connect with a Tampines property specialist for a no-obligation CMA.

STEP 3

Commission a Formal Bank Valuation When Needed

A formal bank valuation (typically $250–$500) is required when you're refinancing, when a buyer's bank finances the purchase, or when you need a legally defensible figure for estate or legal purposes. This is conducted by a panel valuer appointed by the financial institution. You don't typically need to commission this independently — it's triggered as part of a loan application or resale transaction. If you're refinancing using home equity, ask your bank to initiate the process.
